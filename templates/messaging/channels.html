WhatsApp Business Platform Guide
Using the WhatsApp Business App on this number?

To use it with the CRM, you must migrate it to the WhatsApp Business Platform (Cloud API). Once migrated, you will manage all conversations directly from the CRM.

Why migrate to the Cloud API?
  • Multi-agent support — your whole team can reply from the unified inbox.
  • Unified inbox — all channels (WhatsApp, Messenger, etc.) in one place.
  • Team collaboration — assign, share notes, and hand off conversations.
  • Customer management — unified profiles with full conversation history.
  • CRM integration — orders, tags, and customer data at your fingertips.
  • Automation — auto-replies, templates, and workflow triggers.
  • Order & conversation management — track every customer journey.

The CRM replaces the WhatsApp Business mobile app for this phone number after migration.

Step-by-step migration guide

Open the WhatsApp Business App → Settings → Chats → Chat Backup → Back Up. Migration permanently transfers the number to the Cloud API.

In the WhatsApp Business App, go to Settings → Account → Delete my account. Enter your phone number and confirm. This deregisters the number so Meta's Cloud API can claim it.
Important: do this on the old phone only. Skipping this step causes a "number already registered" error later.

Go to Meta Business Settings → WhatsApp Manager. Under Phone Numbers, click Add phone number and follow the wizard. Select your WhatsApp Business Account (WABA) and verify the number via SMS or call.

From the WhatsApp Manager, copy:
  • Phone Number ID — from the Phone Numbers list.
  • WABA ID — from Business Settings → Business Information.
  • Permanent Access Token — from Meta Business Settings → Users → System Users → Generate token.
Close this guide, click Connect on the WhatsApp card, and paste the values.

In Meta → App Dashboard → WhatsApp → Configuration, paste the Webhook URL and Verify Token shown on the channel card, then subscribe to messages.
Want to switch back to the Business App?

You are never locked into the Cloud API. To revert:

  1. Disconnect the number in this CRM (Channels → Disconnect).
  2. In the Meta Business Settings, remove or deregister the number from the Cloud API / WABA.
  3. Install the WhatsApp Business App on your phone and register with the same number.

Your CRM conversation history is preserved; only live WhatsApp messaging stops.

Facebook Messenger Setup Guide
Connect a Facebook Page to the CRM

Once connected, every message your Page receives on Facebook or Messenger appears in the unified inbox — and your whole team can reply from the CRM.

What you get
  • Multi-agent inbox — multiple team members reply to the same Page.
  • Unified inbox — Messenger + WhatsApp + others in one view.
  • Customer profiles — every chat attached to a unified customer record.
  • Notes & assignments — hand off conversations internally.
  • Automation — auto-replies and template messages.
  • Order context — see orders and tags while chatting.

The CRM takes over messaging for this Page. Messages still arrive in your Facebook Inbox too, but replies from the CRM are delivered through the Page.

Step-by-step setup

Go to the Meta for Developers dashboard → My Apps → Create App. Choose the Business type and add the Messenger product.

Open your Facebook Page → About → Page transparency. The Page ID at the bottom is the numeric ID you paste as the "Facebook Page Numeric ID" in the connect form.

In the Meta App dashboard → Messenger → Settings → Access Tokens, select your Page and generate a token with these permissions: pages_messaging, pages_manage_metadata, pages_read_engagement, pages_show_list.
Tip: paste a short-lived user token or the Page token into the connect form and the CRM will exchange it for a long-lived Page token automatically and refresh it in the background.

In the Meta App dashboard → Settings → Basic, copy the App Secret. The CRM uses it to verify that incoming webhook events genuinely came from Meta.

In the Meta App dashboard → Messenger → Settings → Webhooks, paste the Webhook URL and Verify Token from the channel card. Subscribe to the messages, messaging_postbacks, and message_deliveries fields.
Token lifecycle

The CRM stores long-lived Page tokens and checks their health daily. If a token ever expires or is revoked, the channel is marked Expired and you'll be prompted to reconnect — your conversation history stays intact.

Instagram Direct Setup Guide
Connect an Instagram Professional account to the CRM

Instagram DMs run on Meta's Messenger Platform, so setup mirrors the Messenger flow. Once connected, every DM your account receives appears in the unified inbox — and your team can reply from the CRM.

Requirements. You need an Instagram Business or Creator account linked to a Facebook Page. Personal accounts cannot use the messaging API.
What you get
  • Multi-agent inbox — your whole team can reply to the same DMs.
  • Unified inbox — Instagram + Messenger + WhatsApp in one view.
  • Customer profiles — every chat attached to a unified customer record.
  • Notes & assignments — hand off conversations internally.
  • Automation — auto-replies and template messages.
Step-by-step setup

In the Instagram app → Settings → Account type and tools → Switch to professional account. Then link it to a Facebook Page (Settings → Business/Creator → Linked Facebook Page).

Go to the Meta for Developers dashboard → My Apps → Create App. Choose the Business type and add the Messenger product. Under its settings, find the Instagram section and add the test account.

In the Meta App dashboard → Messenger → Settings → Access Tokens, select the Instagram account to surface its ig-user-id. This is what you paste as the "Instagram Account ID" in the connect form.

Generate a Page Access Token (or a user token — the CRM will exchange it for a long-lived one) with these permissions: instagram_basic, instagram_manage_messages, pages_show_list, pages_messaging.

Copy the App Secret from Settings → Basic. In Messenger → Settings → Webhooks, paste the Webhook URL and Verify Token from the channel card and subscribe to the messages, messaging_postbacks and message_deliveries fields for the Instagram account.
The 24-hour messaging window

Instagram (like Messenger) only allows free-form replies within 24 hours of the customer's last message. Outside that window you must use a pre-approved message template. The CRM surfaces this clearly in the composer.

Telegram Bot Setup Guide
Connect a Telegram bot to the CRM

Telegram bots are created with @BotFather. There's no OAuth flow — the bot token is the credential. The CRM auto-registers the webhook with Telegram on connect.

What you get
  • Multi-agent inbox — your whole team replies from the same bot.
  • Unified inbox — Telegram + Messenger + WhatsApp in one view.
  • Rich media — photos, videos, documents, stickers, voice notes, locations.
  • Buttons — inline keyboard taps are surfaced as messages.
  • Customer profiles — every chat attached to a unified customer record.
Step-by-step setup

Open @BotFather in Telegram and send /newbot. Choose a display name and a unique @username ending in bot. BotFather replies with a bot token like 123456789:ABC-DEF….

Send /setwebhook to BotFather, or use the Bot API directly — but it's easier to let the CRM register the webhook on connect. For added security, pick a random string and paste it as the Webhook Secret Token field in the connect form: Telegram will echo it back in every webhook for verification.

Click Connect on the Telegram card and paste the bot token. The "Bot ID" field is the numeric prefix of the token (everything before the colon) — leave it blank and the CRM fills it in automatically.

On connect, the CRM calls Telegram's setWebhook with the per-account webhook URL shown on the channel card. No manual webhook subscription is needed. If you'd rather register it yourself, leave the Webhook URL field blank and call setWebhook manually.

Telegram bots can't message users first — a customer must press /start in your bot once. After that, every message they send lands in the unified inbox and your team can reply freely.
Privacy & limitations

Bots cannot see message delivery/read receipts for private chats, and they can only message users who have started a conversation. The CRM stores your full conversation history even if the bot is later reconnected or renamed.

TikTok Business Setup Guide
Connect a TikTok Business account to the CRM

TikTok's Business Messaging API (on the TikTok for Developers platform) uses standard OAuth 2.0. You authorize the CRM app and paste the access/refresh tokens it returns; the CRM refreshes them automatically before they expire.

Requirements. You need a TikTok Business Center account and a TikTok for Developers app with the Business Messaging scope approved. Access to the Messaging API is currently rolled out regionally.
What you get
  • Multi-agent inbox — your whole team replies to the same TikTok DMs.
  • Unified inbox — TikTok + Messenger + WhatsApp + more in one view.
  • Rich media — images, videos, files and quick replies.
  • Customer profiles — every chat attached to a unified customer record.
  • Token auto-refresh — long-lived refresh tokens keep the channel alive.
Step-by-step setup

Sign in at the TikTok for Developers portal and create an app. Copy the Client Key and Client Secret from the app's basic settings.

Under Scopes, add business.msg.send and the related read/profile scopes, then submit for review. TikTok enables messaging for your app once approved.

Authorize the merchant's TikTok Business account via the OAuth 2.0 Authorization Code flow (your connect UI or a temporary script can do this). Exchange the code at /v2/oauth/token/ to obtain the access_token (~24h) and refresh_token (~30 days).

Open the TikTok Business Center → Business Account Info. The Business ID shown there is what you paste as the "Business ID" in the connect form.

Click Connect on the TikTok card and paste the access token, refresh token, client key and client secret. Then in the TikTok for Developers dashboard → Webhooks, paste the Webhook URL from the channel card. The client secret is used to verify the X-TT-Webhook-Signature HMAC header on every event.
Token lifecycle

TikTok access tokens expire after ~24 hours, but the paired refresh token lasts ~30 days. The CRM refreshes the access token automatically before it expires and persists the new pair — you'll only need to re-authorize if the refresh token itself lapses.

Channel Setup Help

Every channel connects the same way: you provide credentials from the platform, and the CRM handles messaging, webhooks, and token refresh in the background.

General steps
  1. Create an app/account on the platform's developer portal.
  2. Generate an access token and copy your account ID.
  3. Click Connect on the channel card and paste the values.
  4. Register the Webhook URL and Verify Token shown on the card.
Need a specific channel's guide? Use the Setup Guides menu at the top right to open the WhatsApp, Messenger, Instagram, Telegram, or TikTok guide.
