WhatsApp Business Platform Guide
Using the WhatsApp Business App on this number?

To use it with the CRM, you must migrate it to the WhatsApp Business Platform (Cloud API). Once migrated, you will manage all conversations directly from the CRM.

Why migrate to the Cloud API?
  • Multi-agent support — your whole team can reply from the unified inbox.
  • Unified inbox — all channels (WhatsApp, Messenger, etc.) in one place.
  • Team collaboration — assign, share notes, and hand off conversations.
  • Customer management — unified profiles with full conversation history.
  • CRM integration — orders, tags, and customer data at your fingertips.
  • Automation — auto-replies, templates, and workflow triggers.
  • Order & conversation management — track every customer journey.

The CRM replaces the WhatsApp Business mobile app for this phone number after migration.

Step-by-step migration guide

Open the WhatsApp Business App → Settings → Chats → Chat Backup → Back Up. Migration permanently transfers the number to the Cloud API.

In the WhatsApp Business App, go to Settings → Account → Delete my account. Enter your phone number and confirm. This deregisters the number so Meta's Cloud API can claim it.
Important: do this on the old phone only. Skipping this step causes a "number already registered" error later.

Go to Meta Business Settings → WhatsApp Manager. Under Phone Numbers, click Add phone number and follow the wizard. Select your WhatsApp Business Account (WABA) and verify the number via SMS or call.

From the WhatsApp Manager, copy:
  • Phone Number ID — from the Phone Numbers list.
  • WABA ID — from Business Settings → Business Information.
  • Permanent Access Token — from Meta Business Settings → Users → System Users → Generate token.
Close this guide, click Connect on the WhatsApp card, and paste the values.

In Meta → App Dashboard → WhatsApp → Configuration, paste the Webhook URL and Verify Token shown on the channel card, then subscribe to messages.
Want to switch back to the Business App?

You are never locked into the Cloud API. To revert:

  1. Disconnect the number in this CRM (Channels → Disconnect).
  2. In the Meta Business Settings, remove or deregister the number from the Cloud API / WABA.
  3. Install the WhatsApp Business App on your phone and register with the same number.

Your CRM conversation history is preserved; only live WhatsApp messaging stops.

Facebook Messenger Setup Guide
Connect a Facebook Page to the CRM

Once connected, every message your Page receives on Facebook or Messenger appears in the unified inbox — and your whole team can reply from the CRM.

What you get
  • Multi-agent inbox — multiple team members reply to the same Page.
  • Unified inbox — Messenger + WhatsApp + others in one view.
  • Customer profiles — every chat attached to a unified customer record.
  • Notes & assignments — hand off conversations internally.
  • Automation — auto-replies and template messages.
  • Order context — see orders and tags while chatting.

The CRM takes over messaging for this Page. Messages still arrive in your Facebook Inbox too, but replies from the CRM are delivered through the Page.

Step-by-step setup

Go to the Meta for Developers dashboard → My Apps → Create App. Choose the Business type and add the Messenger product.

Open your Facebook Page → About → Page transparency. The Page ID at the bottom is the numeric ID you paste as the "Facebook Page Numeric ID" in the connect form.

In the Meta App dashboard → Messenger → Settings → Access Tokens, select your Page and generate a token with these permissions: pages_messaging, pages_manage_metadata, pages_read_engagement, pages_show_list.
Tip: paste a short-lived user token or the Page token into the connect form and the CRM will exchange it for a long-lived Page token automatically and refresh it in the background.

In the Meta App dashboard → Settings → Basic, copy the App Secret. The CRM uses it to verify that incoming webhook events genuinely came from Meta.

In the Meta App dashboard → Messenger → Settings → Webhooks, paste the Webhook URL and Verify Token from the channel card. Subscribe to the messages, messaging_postbacks, and message_deliveries fields.
Token lifecycle

The CRM stores long-lived Page tokens and checks their health daily. If a token ever expires or is revoked, the channel is marked Expired and you'll be prompted to reconnect — your conversation history stays intact.

Channel Setup Help

Every channel connects the same way: you provide credentials from the platform, and the CRM handles messaging, webhooks, and token refresh in the background.

General steps
  1. Create an app/account on the platform's developer portal.
  2. Generate an access token and copy your account ID.
  3. Click Connect on the channel card and paste the values.
  4. Register the Webhook URL and Verify Token shown on the card.
Need a specific channel's guide? Use the Setup Guides menu at the top right to open the WhatsApp or Messenger guide.
